THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA

The USA uses advanced, but oftentimes experimental, technology with precise targeting and well trained infantry. It also commands a powerful air force, which includes the F-22 Raptor and the Aurora strike bomber. The USA's superweapon of choice, the Particle Cannon, allows for more precise and continuous destruction than other factions' crude, area-blast weapons. With a vast amount of intelligence-gathering resources available, US commanders are scarcely seen making any blundering errors when engaging the enemy, regularly picking the correct weak spots as indicated by their satellite scans. Most of the USA's units are of high quality, despite more expensive than their Chinese counterparts; even their basic infantry unit, the Ranger is able to defeat its Chinese and GLA counterparts. While their high technology greatly augments the USA's fighting capabilities, it comes at very high costs. This, combined with their slow economy, forces the USA to field a smaller and more specialized army.

GENERAL "PINPOINT" TOWNES

A champion of leading-edge technology, General "Pinpoint" Townes has developed comprehensive laser-based weapons strategies. Generals Townes uses lasers throughout his forces, mounting them on both defensive turrets and tanks. He continues to make improvements in power efficiency in order to harness energy for his plethora of lasers.

  • Armed with new laser weaponry.
  • Avengers cost less.
  • Tomahawk missile is unavailable.
  • The Crusader tank and Paladin tank are both unavailable.

GENERAL ALEXIS ALEXANDER

Specializing in Weapons of Mass Destruction, General Alexis Alexander is a distinguished Marine Corps general. Alexander has developed a defensive scheme that has the goal of keeping supply lines in tact. This General's main priority in the early phase of conflict is defense and resource gathering.

  • Particle Cannons cost half the normal price.
  • All vehicles cost more to produce.
  • The Crusader tank and Paladin tank are both unavailable.

GENERAL MALCOLM "ACE" GRANGER

General Malcolm Granger is the even-tempered yet uncompromising son of an Iowa crop duster. His career in flight began at an early age. During the Vietnam War, Granger, then a lieutenant, became an ace in a single day by shooting down 4 MiGs. Having advanced in the Air Force ranks, Granger now specializes in advancing the role of America's military aircraft.

  • All air units have laser-point defense systems, except for the Comanche.
  • All aircraft cost less.
  • The Stealth Fighter no longer requires a General's point upgrade. It is available from the start.
  • The Crusader tank and Paladin tank are both unavailable.
